What I kept watching.

I have sat across from patients who cover their mouths when they laugh. It is the hardest thing to watch as a dentist.

In my practice, I hear the same story every week. A patient comes in. They hate their smile. They have stopped being in photos with their kids. They want it fixed, but the clinical reality is brutal: traditional porcelain veneers mean hours in my chair, permanently drilling down healthy enamel, and a bill between ten and twenty thousand dollars.

For most hardworking people, that is not an option.

So they go online and buy a cheap plastic snap-on for a couple hundred dollars. A few weeks later, they are sitting in my chair again, showing me what arrived. It is always the same. The appliance is stamped from a single mold. The gingival margin, the gumline, is a straight, thick plastic ridge. The teeth look like identical white piano keys. They put it in, realize everyone can immediately tell it is fake, and throw it in a drawer.

They paid good money to feel worse than when they started. And the companies selling these things keep selling them anyway, because the customer is too embarrassed to complain publicly.

I got tired of seeing it. I know exactly what makes a tooth look real. It is not magic. It is proportion, translucency, and how the edge meets the gum. I knew those clinical standards could be engineered into a removable appliance if someone actually did the work. So I did.

Every Revive case is produced against the 7-Point Smile Design Protocol I built. If a case does not pass my standard, it does not ship.

Let me be clear about one thing. I am a cosmetic dentist, but I am not your dentist, and Revive is not dental treatment. I designed a product.

I designed the standard behind it too, so a more natural-looking appliance could be made for people who had almost given up on their smile.

The Protocol

Seven checkpoints that make them look like your own teeth.

Every Revive case is designed against a 7-point protocol I built, and it has to pass all seven before it leaves the facility. Tap any point to see what it decides.

We map the design to your specific facial proportions, not a template. Your lip line and arch width dictate the shape, ensuring the final result actually belongs on your face.

If the appliance fights your natural bite, you will not wear it. We engineer the alignment so your upper and lower teeth meet correctly, making the set comfortable enough for daily use.

Natural teeth are not identical tiles. We size and shape each tooth individually in proportion to the ones next to it. That slight, deliberate variation is what makes a smile look real.

This is where cheap snap-ons fail. We contour the edge where the veneer meets your gum exactly to your anatomy. It eliminates the thick plastic line that instantly gives away a fake appliance.

Real enamel is slightly see-through at the incisal edge (the biting edge). We replicate that translucency so your teeth catch light naturally, rather than looking like a solid block of white plastic.

The color must be bright but believable. We select a shade that looks natural for your complexion, avoiding the fluorescent white that tells everyone you are wearing something artificial.

Before anything ships, the finished case is checked against the six design decisions above. With this final inspection, that is seven checkpoints in total. If the case misses on any of them, it does not go out. That is the point of putting my name on it.

If it does not pass all seven, it does not leave the facility.
